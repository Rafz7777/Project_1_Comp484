Restoration Progress

This section highlights the progress made over the years. After bringing the car home, it was completely disassembled, inventoried, and stored for later reassembly. The vehicle was then taken to a sandblasting shop, where all paint and rust were removed, and an etching primer was applied to prevent future corrosion. Certain components, including the front-end steering assembly and the rear-end assembly, were powder coated immediately for protection.

Rust repair was a major focus. The most affected areas included the floorpans, running boards, spare tire wheel well, and other smaller sections where rust had penetrated. Fortunately, this car was originally a California vehicle, sparing it from the severe rust damage common to many imported Vauxhalls. Once the damaged areas were patched, welding was completed, and additional etching primer was applied to prevent rust from reoccurring.

With the rust issues addressed, work moved on to straightening the body and applying an initial primer coat. A layer of yellow primer was used first, which makes block sanding easier and helps ensure a smooth finish. The final paint coat will be one of the original 1957 Vauxhall finishes: Empress Blue.

Engine Hunt

This section highlights the journey I took to find an original Vauxhall engine to rebuild for my restoration. I found a donor engine along with other parts that I can use to restore my Vauxhall to original specifications. The car was located in Canada and I had to disassemble in freezing conditions! The temperature fell to below negative 10 degrees so it was quite the chore to disassemble in the bitter cold.

Engine Rebuild

Rebuilding the engine step by step with new and refurbished parts. The following images depict the rebuilt engine and painstaking work. The engine was completely disassembled and rebuilt with parts from the UK and the US. The final engine build was tested and ran on a custom engine stand to truly test all functionality before placing in the car. More images will be added which shows the many steps taken to complete the work. More to come...
