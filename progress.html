Restoration Progress

This section highlights some progress that has been done over the years. The car was brought home and completely disassembled, inventoried, and stored for later assembly as needed. Afterwards, the car was taken to a sandblasting shop and completely stripped of all paint and rust finishing with an etching primer to prevent future rust build-up. A select amount of components were powder coated right away, such as the front-end steering assembly as well as the rear-end assembly.

The car then needed to be patched up where rust had did the most damage. These areas were the floorpans inside the vehicle, running boards, spare tire wheel well, and other small areas where rust had eaten through. The fact that this was an original California car spared this car the fate of most Vauxhalls that were imported to the US: Death by rust corrosion! After the rust issues were taken care of, welding was completed by adding more etching primer to prevent rust reoccurring.

After welding and patching the severest rust issues, work commenced on straightening out the body and applying an initial primer coat. The car is being painted first in a coat of yellow primer to make the forthcoming block sanding easier to see and work with. The final paint coat will be one of the original finishes for this year of Vauxhall: Empress Blue.

Engine Hunt

This section highlights the journey I took to find an original Vauxhall engine to rebuild for my restoration. I found a donor engine along with other parts that I can use to restore my Vauxhall to original specifications. The car was located in Canada and I had to disassemble in the botter cold! The temperature fell to below negative 10 degrees so it was quite the chore to disassemble in the bitter cold.

Engine Rebuild

Rebuilding the engine step by step with new and refurbished parts. The following images depict the rebuilt engine and painstaking work. The engine was completely disassembled and rebuilt with parts from the UK and the US. The final engine build was tested and ran on a custom engine stand to truly test all functionality before placing in the car. More images will be added which shows the many steps taken to complete the work. More to come...
